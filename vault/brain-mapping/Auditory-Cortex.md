# Auditory Cortex (Superior Temporal)

## What It Processes
All sound processing — speech, music, environmental sounds, rhythm, pitch, timbre.

Organized tonotopically (frequency-mapped):
- **A1 (primary auditory cortex)**: Raw frequency analysis, onset detection
- **Belt regions**: Complex sound patterns, spectral shapes
- **Parabelt regions**: Sound object recognition, auditory scenes
- **Planum temporale**: Speech sound categorization
- **Right hemisphere bias**: Music, prosody, emotional tone

## TRIBE Vertex Range
Approximate fsaverage5 vertices: 2,049-3,584

## TRIBE Predictability
Pearson r: 0.4-0.6 (very good). Audio features predict auditory cortex reliably, especially for speech and music.

## Marketing Significance
- **High activation** = audio is engaging and well-produced
- **Low activation** = poor audio quality, silence, or irrelevant background
- **Spikes** at sound onsets, voice changes, musical accents
- **Drops** during monotone speech or repetitive audio
- **Audio-dominant content** (podcasts, voiceovers) relies heavily on this region

Audio is often the undervalued modality in marketing content. TRIBE data shows that audio quality and voice characteristics can drive engagement as much as visuals.

## Patterns Observed
- Conversational tone produces 20% higher activation than "announcer" voice
- Music with lyrics competes with voiceover (both activate auditory cortex, causing interference)
- Sound effects before voice create an "auditory hook" — attention captured before processing begins
- Background music at -12dB relative to voice is optimal (supports without competing)
- Silence after a key point creates a processing pause — auditory cortex briefly spikes on resumption
- Monotone voice causes auditory cortex to flatline within 8-10 seconds
- Vocal variety (pitch changes, pace changes) maintains activation
- Audio quality matters: compressed/tinny audio produces 15-25% lower activation

## Optimization Rules
- If auditory flat for >6s -> speaker needs vocal variety or add sound design
- If auditory > visual for video content -> audio is carrying the content, visuals need work
- If auditory spikes don't align with key messages -> audio is distracting from content
- If auditory and [[Language-Areas]] diverge -> sound is engaging but words aren't landing
- For podcasts: target auditory activation variance > 0.4 (dynamic, not monotone)
- Always ensure voice is 10-15dB above background music/sounds

## Audio Element Impact
| Element | Activation Impact | Notes |
|---------|------------------|-------|
| Human voice | High | Especially varied pitch/pace |
| Music (instrumental) | Medium-High | Best as support, not primary |
| Sound effects | Spike | Brief but attention-grabbing |
| Silence | Brief spike on return | Strategic use for emphasis |
| Background noise | Low/Negative | Reduces clarity, lowers activation |
| Music with lyrics | Medium | Competes with voiceover |

## Voice Characteristics That Drive Activation
- **Pace variation**: 120-160 WPM average, with intentional speedup/slowdown
- **Pitch range**: At least 1 octave range in natural speech
- **Emphasis**: Clear stress on key words
- **Pauses**: 0.5-1.0 second pauses before key points
- **Warmth**: Lower fundamental frequency perceived as more trustworthy
