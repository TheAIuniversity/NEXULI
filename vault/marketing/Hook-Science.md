# Hook Science — The First 3 Seconds

## Why Hooks Matter
Social media scroll speed: users decide in 1-3 seconds whether to stop.
TRIBE shows exactly what brain regions fire in those first seconds.

Average scroll speed by platform:
- TikTok: 1.2 seconds per item
- Instagram Reels: 1.5 seconds per item
- YouTube Shorts: 1.8 seconds per item
- Twitter/X feed: 0.8 seconds per item
- LinkedIn feed: 2.1 seconds per item

If you don't capture attention in that window, your content never gets processed by the deeper brain regions that drive action.

## What Makes a Strong Hook (from TRIBE data)

### Face-First (Fusiform Face Area)
- Human face in frame 1 triggers immediate fusiform activation
- Direct eye contact > profile view
- Emotional expression > neutral face
- Close-up > wide shot
- TRIBE data: Face in first 2 frames produces +40% [[Fusiform-Face-Area]] activation vs. no-face opening

### Question Opening (Prefrontal Cortex)
- Questions spike prefrontal activation (evaluation mode)
- "What if..." patterns strongest
- Specific > vague ("What if your ads scored themselves?" > "What if things were better?")
- TRIBE data: Questions produce +35% [[Prefrontal-Cortex]] activation vs. statements in first 3s

### Pattern Interrupt (Visual Cortex)
- Unexpected visual element spikes V1/V2
- Movement from stillness
- Contrast change (dark to bright)
- Text appearing with motion
- TRIBE data: High-contrast scene changes in first second produce +28% [[Visual-Cortex]] spike

### Audio Hook (Auditory Cortex)
- Voice starting immediately > music intro
- Conversational tone > announcer voice
- Sound effect before voice grabs auditory cortex first
- TRIBE data: Voice in first 0.5s produces +22% [[Auditory-Cortex]] activation vs. music-first intro

## Hook Score Benchmarks
- Below 40: Weak hook — likely scroll-past
- 40-60: Average — works for warm audiences
- 60-80: Strong — will stop most thumbs
- Above 80: Exceptional — viral potential

## Hook Patterns Ranked by TRIBE Activation

### Tier 1: Highest Activation (Hook Score 75+)
1. **Face + Question + Audio**: Close-up face, direct eye contact, asking a specific question, voice immediate. Activates FFA + PFC + auditory simultaneously.
2. **Pattern Interrupt + Face**: Unexpected visual, then face appears. Visual cortex spike chains into FFA activation.
3. **Emotional Face + Sound Effect**: Strong emotional expression with a punchy sound effect. FFA + auditory + DMN all within 1 second.

### Tier 2: Strong Activation (Hook Score 55-75)
4. **Question Text Overlay + Voice**: Text appears with motion while voice reads it. Language areas + visual + auditory.
5. **Action/Motion + Voice**: Something happening visually with immediate narration. Visual + auditory.
6. **Before/After Visual**: Split screen or rapid transition showing transformation. Visual cortex + DMN (self-projection).

### Tier 3: Moderate Activation (Hook Score 40-55)
7. **Music + Text**: Trending audio with text overlay. Audio + visual, but no face = lower engagement.
8. **Landscape/Product + Voice**: Product shot or scene with voiceover. Decent but no face anchor.
9. **Title Card + Music**: Branded intro. Familiar for warm audiences but low activation for cold.

### Tier 4: Weak Activation (Hook Score <40)
10. **Logo Intro**: Animated logo reveal. Only activates visual cortex briefly. Kills cold audience engagement.
11. **Slow Fade In**: Gradual visual appearance. No spike to capture attention.
12. **Music-Only Intro**: No voice, no face, no text. Auditory cortex alone cannot hold against scroll.

## Platform-Specific Hook Strategies

### TikTok/Reels (1.2-1.5s decision window)
- Face in frame 1, speaking immediately
- Text hook visible within 0.5s
- Sound on assumed — use audio hook
- Pattern interrupt in first 0.3s if no face

### YouTube (longer window, but thumbnail is the "hook")
- Thumbnail = static hook (optimize with [[Fusiform-Face-Area]])
- First 5 seconds confirm thumbnail promise
- "In this video..." openings are weak (Hook Score ~35)
- Jump straight into the value/hook

### LinkedIn (2.1s window, professional context)
- Strong opening sentence > face (text-dominant platform)
- Data point or surprising stat in first line
- Questions work exceptionally well for [[Prefrontal-Cortex]] activation
- Professional face photo helps but is not primary driver

### Podcast (Audio-only hook)
- Voice starts immediately (no music intro)
- First sentence must be a hook (question, surprising statement, teaser)
- Conversational energy high from word one
- Sound effect "stinger" before voice can work
